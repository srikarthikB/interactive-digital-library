Interactive Digital Library

About the Project

The Interactive Digital Library is a prototype designed to enhance digital reading experiences by integrating AI-generated summaries, visual representations, and optional audio narration.

Why This Matters

Traditional digital reading platforms often provide static text without contextual engagement tools. This platform enhances comprehension and accessibility by combining AI-generated summaries, visual scene interpretations, and optional audio narration to support different learning styles.

⚠ Copyright & Fair Use Notice

This project does not host, store, or distribute copyrighted books. All full texts are accessed through external public platforms such as Archive.org. The AI-generated summaries, visuals, and audio content provided here are intended solely as educational pre-reading aids under fair use principles.

Instead of hosting copyrighted books directly, the platform ethically redirects users to publicly accessible sources while offering AI-powered pre-reading aids to improve comprehension and engagement.

This project was developed as part of an academic Design Thinking & Innovation assignment, focusing on accessibility, ethical content use, and user-centered design.

Key Features

  • AI-generated text summaries for books
  • Optional audio narration using text-to-speech
  • AI-generated visual scene summaries
  • Searchable and responsive book library
  • Ethical redirection to external reading sources

Academic Resources

In addition to literature and epics, the platform also includes academic textbooks categorized by engineering departments such as Computer Science, Electronics, and Mechanical Engineering. These resources demonstrate how the system can support educational content alongside traditional reading.

Limitations of the Current Prototype

  • AI-generated visuals are interpretive representations.
  • Summaries may simplify complex themes from the original text.
  • The prototype currently supports a limited dataset of books.
  • AI content is pre-generated rather than dynamically created.

Future Scope

  • Real-time AI visual generation
  • Integrated in-app reading interface
  • Multi-language summaries and narration
  • Personalized reading recommendations
  • Direct integration with college digital library systems
